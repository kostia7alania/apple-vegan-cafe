# ADR-0004: i18n URL policy — EN root, /th/, /ru/, localized article slugs

Date: 2026-07-15 · Status: accepted

## Context

Launch locales by verified demand: EN (tourists/expats/Indian visitors), TH
(เจ cluster unowned in Thai SERPs), RU (top-3 tourist market, zero
restaurant-owned sites in RU SERPs). Google guidance verified 2026-07-15:
subdirectories recommended; localized/transliterated slugs endorsed; no
Accept-Language auto-redirects (Googlebot crawls from US); reciprocal hreflang
required, one placement method.

## Decision

- `/` = full English page and `x-default`; `/th/…`, `/ru/…` subdirectories.
  No IP/language redirects ever — at most a suggestion banner (later).
- Structural pages share English slugs (`/menu/`, `/contact/`, `/about/`,
  `/blog/`). Articles/landing pages use per-language slugs (RU transliterated
  Latin; Thai script allowed for Thai-targeted pages) linked by
  `translationKey`; old slugs go to `redirects.json` → `_redirects` (301).
- hreflang: `<head>` only (incl. self + x-default), generated from
  translation sets; the sitemap stays plain. Language switcher = crawlable
  `<a>` links; untranslated pages are simply absent (no fallback routing).
- Content policy: every published language version is human-reviewed; no
  machine-translation-at-scale (named spam by Google), no doorway area pages.
  Phase-2 locales (zh/ko/de/fr) launch only with a native reviewer.

## Consequences

Adding a locale = add it to `LOCALES`, translate content, review. No
architectural change. Head terms are targeted by the home page only —
learned from the competitor's landing-page cannibalization.

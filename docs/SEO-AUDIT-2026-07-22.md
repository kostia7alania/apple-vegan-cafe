## Current Google guidance on links

The old idea that every external link "leaks SEO juice" is too crude now.

Current practical guidance:

- Important pages should be linked from at least one crawlable internal link.
- Internal links should help users and Google understand page relationships.
- Anchor text should be natural and descriptive, not keyword-stuffed.
- Links should have context around them.
- Too many adjacent links are a weak UX/content signal; Google does not publish an ideal number.
- Relevant outbound links are not automatically bad. They can support trust when they are useful and contextual.
- `nofollow` is for untrusted, paid, user-generated, or non-endorsed links. Normal business/social/order links should not be nofollowed just to "save weight".

Conclusion for this site: do not build artificial link rings. Use contextual internal links where they help a visitor choose food, order, or understand vegan/Jay/Jain details.

### 5. External links are useful, but the pattern should stay intentional

Current repeated outbound links are mostly business CTAs: GrabFood, HappyCow, Instagram. These are not inherently bad. The risk is not "external links", it is repeated low-context links that look like noise.
